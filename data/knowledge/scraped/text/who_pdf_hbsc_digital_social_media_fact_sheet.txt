CHILD AND ADOLESCENT HEAL TH IN THE WHO EUROPEAN REGION:  
DIGITAL ENVIRONMENT FACT SHEET

 y  One in three adolescents maintain continuous online 
contact with friends and others. Social media use 
increases with age and is higher in adolescents from 
more affluent families.
 y  Problematic social media use has risen from 7%  
in 2018 to 11% in 2022 and varies across countries.
 y  One in three adolescents play digital games every 
day, and one in five play for at least four hours  
on a gaming day.
 y  Problematic social media use is more common in 
girls; problematic gaming is more common in boys.
 y  Digital media use starts early; only one in four 
children under two years of age and one in three 
children age two to five meet international screen 
time guidelines.
WHY IS THIS IMPORTANT?
 y  Digital technologies play a central role in 
adolescents’ social lives, education, and 
entertainment. However, a growing number of 
adolescents seem to use social media and digital 
gaming in a problematic way.
 y  Problematic social media use is linked to 
mental health issues, reduced well being, sleep 
disturbances, and increased substance abuse.
 y  Problematic gaming is associated with lower 
life satisfaction, more frequent psychological 
complaints, and decreased peer support.
 y  These digital behaviours can impact physical health 
and academic performance.
 y  Early problematic use may lead to long term 
behavioural patterns that affect adult life.
SUCCESSFUL GOVERNANCE:
 y  Regulates and safeguards against the emerging 
harmful influence of digital technologies.
 y  Develops regulatory frameworks that ensure age 
appropriate content and protect against harmful 
marketing by holding the industry accountable for 
monitoring and enforcing age restrictions on their 
products.
 y  Considers the challenges of the digital age in health 
and education policy so that the balance between 
online activities and offline pursuits is embedded  
in children’s and adolescents’ understanding of good 
health and wellbeing.
 y  Introduces screen time policies in line with WHO 
guidelines on sedentary behaviour.
 y  Ensures robust parental and educational guidance 
so that the problematic use of digital technology 
is understood and at-risk adolescents can receive 
support.
 y  Introduces digital literacy programmes starting from 
early childhood.
 y  Establishes clear policies for smartphone use in 
school environments.
